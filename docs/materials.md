Veydra‚Äôs material strategy is designed to balance structural performance, manufacturing feasibility, cost, and long-term sustainability.  
Rather than relying on exotic or speculative materials, Veydra uses a **mixed-material architecture** optimized for stiffness, crash safety, mass reduction, and recyclability.

This document outlines the materials selected for Veydra‚Äôs structural core, body systems, modules, and interior components.

# üß± **1. Materials Philosophy**

### **1.1 Use the Right Material in the Right Place**
Veydra does not rely solely on one material type. Instead, it uses:
- Aluminum for structural efficiency  
- High-strength steel where crash loads require it  
- Composites for lightweight stiffness  
- Recyclable polymers for non-structural parts  

### **1.2 Reduce Mass Without Compromising Safety**
Lightweighting is done through:
- Structural integration (battery-as-frame)  
- Material optimization  
- Modular design that avoids redundant structure  

### **1.3 Prioritize Recyclability**
Materials are selected for:
- End-of-life recovery  
- Low-environmental-impact manufacturing  
- Reuse in second-life applications

# üß© **2. Structural Materials**

The core of Veydra‚Äôs body structure consists of a hybrid framework built around the **structural battery floor**.

---

## **2.1 Aluminum Extrusions**
Used for:
- Side sills  
- Front and rear crossmembers  
- Roof rails  
- Door surround structure  

### Benefits:
- High stiffness-to-weight ratio  
- Predictable crash behavior  
- Highly recyclable  
- Compatible with structural adhesives + mechanical fasteners  

---

## **2.2 High-Strength Steel (HSS)**
Used selectively in:
- A, B, and C pillars  
- Crash rails  
- Rollover-critical zones  

### Benefits:
- High energy absorption  
- Protects occupant cell  
- Cost-effective  
- Good joining compatibility with aluminum when properly isolated  

---

## **2.3 Composite Panels**
Used in:
- Underbody aero/impact panel  
- Rear pods  
- Roof modules (aero pod, solar pod)  
- Certain exterior panels  

### Benefits:
- Low mass  
- Good stiffness  
- Flexible shaping  
- Corrosion-resistant  

Composites are placed where:
- High strength is needed  
- Weight reduction has strong aero/range benefit  
- Structural load is lower than in metal regions  

---

# üîã **3. Battery Enclosure Materials**

The structural battery floor uses a sandwich construction.

## **3.1 Upper Skin**
- Aluminum or composite  
- Must support interior loads  
- Forms integrated cabin floor  

## **3.2 Core Structure**
- Battery modules  
- Aluminum ribs  
- Thermal channels  
- Fire-resistant separators  

## **3.3 Lower Skin**
- Composite with ceramic reinforcement  
- Provides impact resistance  
- Serves as aerodynamic undertray  

The result is a chassis that is:
- Strong  
- Lightweight  
- Thermally stable  
- Highly rigid

# üèóÔ∏è **4. Body Exterior Materials**

### **4.1 Sheet-Metal Aluminum**
Used for:
- Doors  
- Hood (if present)  
- Fenders  

Chose over steel due to:
- Lower mass  
- Easier shaping for aero  
- No corrosion issues  

---

### **4.2 Thermoplastic and Fiber-Reinforced Plastics (FRP)**
Used for:
- Bumpers  
- Fascias  
- Wheel arch trim  
- Some rear pod components  

Advantages:
- Lightweight  
- Flexible under minor impact  
- High surface finish quality  

---

### **4.3 Glass & Polycarbonate**
Glazing materials include:
- Laminated glass (front/rear)  
- Polycarbonate options for roof modules  

Polycarbonate is used only where:
- Weight reduction is critical  
- UV and scratch protection coatings can be applied
