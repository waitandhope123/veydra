# ü™Ç **3. Adaptive AeroBrake‚Ñ¢ System**

The **Adaptive AeroBrake‚Ñ¢** is an active aerodynamic safety system designed to increase drag and stability during emergency deceleration.

Unlike spoilers or wings meant for performance, Veydra‚Äôs system is optimized for **safety and control**.

---

## **3.1 Purpose**
The system provides:

- **Additional drag** to assist emergency braking  
- **Rear-end stability** under high deceleration loads  
- **Improved energy recapture** during hard regen events  
- **Predictive safety activation** in conjunction with sensors  

The goal is to reduce stopping distance and improve control, particularly on low-friction surfaces.

---

## **3.2 How It Works**

### **Normal Driving**
- All aero surfaces remain **fully retracted**  
- Vehicle maintains smooth, low-drag profile  

### **Emergency Braking or Defensive Mode**
Panels rapidly deploy from the rear upper body:

- Increasing frontal area  
- Raising drag coefficient  
- Applying stabilizing downforce  
- Improving rear tracking stability  

Deployment is **computer-controlled** and adjusts based on:
- Vehicle speed  
- Steering angle  
- Traction conditions  
- Regen/friction brake balance  

---

## **3.3 Activation Conditions**

The AeroBrake may activate when:
- Rapid brake pedal depression is detected  
- Forward collision sensors predict impact risk  
- Defensive Mode is manually engaged  
- Traction control senses instability  

The system only deploys when safe and retracts automatically when conditions normalize.

---

## **3.4 System Components**

- **Dual or triple-panel deployable surfaces**  
- **Electromechanical actuators** tied into braking ECU  
- **Load-bearing hinges** designed for aerodynamic forces  
- **Position sensors** ensuring correct deployment  
- **Logic controller** coordinating regen, ABS, ESC, and AeroBrake
