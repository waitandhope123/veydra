# **Veydra ‚Äì Architecture & Platform Overview**

Veydra is built on a clean-sheet, EV-native architecture with the structural battery pack serving as the foundation of the chassis. Every major component ‚Äî suspension, motors, body structure, crash systems ‚Äî is designed around this energy floor.

This document outlines the platform layout, load paths, structural philosophy, and core engineering concepts behind the Veydra architecture.

## üß± **1. Structural Battery Floor**

### **1.1 Concept**
The battery is not a cargo box bolted under the car.  
It *is* the car‚Äôs primary structural element.

The pack is built as a **sandwich beam**, consisting of:
- Upper floor skin (interior surface)
- Structural core containing modules, ribs, and thermal channels
- Lower impact-resistant underbody skin

This provides:
- High bending stiffness  
- High torsional rigidity  
- Low center of gravity  
- A completely flat interior floor  

The structural battery eliminates the need for:
- Transmission tunnels  
- Large front engine bays  
- Heavy ladder- or frame-style structures

## üèóÔ∏è **2. Platform Perimeter Structure**

Surrounding the energy floor is a rigid perimeter frame:

### **2.1 Side Sills**
Tall, multi-chamber sills:
- Carry crash and bending loads  
- Tie into the A, B, and C pillars  
- Shield the battery from intrusion  
- Provide structural hardpoints for seats, belts, and modules  

### **2.2 Crossmembers**
Front and rear crossmembers:
- Support e-axle mounting  
- Tie into subframes and suspension  
- Route crash loads around the battery zone  

These elements work with the battery floor to create a closed structural loop.

## ‚öôÔ∏è **3. Drivetrain Integration**

### **3.1 E-Axle Modules**
Veydra uses modular e-axles:

- Motor  
- Inverter  
- Reduction gear  
- Differential (if needed)  
- Cooling interfaces  

They bolt directly to reinforced points on the crossmembers.

**Configurations:**
- Rear-wheel drive (standard)
- All-wheel drive (dual e-axle configuration)

### **3.2 Motor Placement**
Motors are mounted *inboard* to:
- Reduce unsprung mass  
- Simplify cooling  
- Lower polar moment for better handling

## üåÄ **4. Suspension & Hardpoints**

Suspension components attach to the perimeter frame, not the battery:

### **Front Suspension**
- Compact double wishbone or MacPherson strut  
- Subframe attached to the front crossmember and sills  

### **Rear Suspension**
- Multi-link independent system  
- Subframe attached to the rear crossmember and sills  

### **Benefits**
- Precise wheel control  
- Reduced NVH  
- Improved crash isolation  
- No suspension loads transmitted through the battery interior  

---

## üî• **5. Thermal Spine Architecture**

Running longitudinally through the pack is a **thermal spine** that contains:
- Coolant manifolds  
- Pumps and valves  
- Thermal fusing  
- Interfaces to motors, inverters, and HVAC  

This keeps thermal routing centralized and easy to service.

---

## üßä **6. Battery Safety & Zoning**

The battery pack is divided into **multiple zones**, each with:
- Local sensors  
- Local disconnect capability  
- Fault isolation devices  
- Temperature boundaries  

In a crash or internal fault:
- Affected zones can be isolated  
- HV system can discharge safely  
- Venting routes gases away from the cabin  

This improves both occupant and pack safety.

---

## üöß **7. Crash Structures**

Crash structures are designed *around* the energy floor.

### **7.1 Front Structure**
- Progressive crush rails  
- Upper load paths routed over the pack  
- Lower load paths routed under the pack  
- Energy steered away from the battery interior  

### **7.2 Side Structure**
- Multi-wall sills  
- Lateral crush zones between sill and battery modules  
- Cross-beams linking left and right sides  

### **7.3 Rear Structure**
- Deformation zones sized to protect rear modules and motors  
- Rear crossmember engineered to prevent intrusion into battery  

All three zones create a balanced crash management system.

## ü™∂ **8. Materials Strategy**

Veydra uses a mixed-material approach to optimize:
- Stiffness  
- Weight  
- Crash energy absorption  
- Manufacturability  

Likely materials include:
- Aluminum extrusions (sills, crossmembers)
- High-strength steel (crash beams, pillars)
- Composites (underbody, roof, certain modules)
- Structural adhesives + mechanical fasteners for hybrid joints

## üß© **9. Body "Top Hat" Structure**

The ‚Äútop hat‚Äù (the body above the floor) is designed to be **modular**:
- Rear modules attach to a standardized rear interface  
- Roof modules attach to integrated roof rails  
- Interior modules lock into floor mounting rails  

The top hat contributes to:
- Aerodynamic form  
- Passenger safety  
- Structural support  

But it is not the primary load-bearing element ‚Äî the floor is.

---

## üß¨ **10. Architectural Summary**

Veydra‚Äôs platform can be summarized as:

- A **structural energy floor** acting as the chassis  
- Reinforced **perimeter frame** handling suspension and crash loads  
- Modular **e-axles** for simple RWD/AWD configurations  
- A centralized **thermal spine**  
- A **modular top structure** for reconfigurable body forms  
- Safety built through **smart structures** and **load routing**, not mass  

This architecture unlocks the full potential of EV design by separating itself from legacy combustion layouts completely.
